      How the algorithm works

      12-1 cross-sectional momentum · three regional sleeves

      Each month, in each region (FTSE / US / ASX), rank stocks by 12-1 momentum. Buy the strongest names that are in an uptrend while the market index is also in an uptrend; otherwise hold cash. Size positions by inverse volatility, then scale the whole book to a 12% volatility target. Run three regional books and report the combined equity in AUD.

      12-1 Momentum

      The last-year return, skipping the most recent month (avoids short-term reversal).

      score(t) = P(t−21) / P(t−252) − 1

      Trend & regime filters

      A stock must be above its 200-day moving average, and the regional index must be above its own 200-day MA. If the index is below, that entire sleeve goes 100% cash (crash protection).

      Selection & weighting

      Take the top N = 10 names by momentum. Assign inverse-volatility weights (calmer names get more), then cap each name at 15% so no single position dominates.

      Volatility targeting

      Scale the whole book so estimated portfolio volatility is about 12% per year. Gross exposure never exceeds 100% — no leverage.

      Costs

      Commission + slippage on turnover at every rebalance, plus UK stamp duty (0.5%) on FTSE buys only. Trades are whole-share lots and respect per-trade commission floors.

      Three sleeves → one AUD book

      Each sleeve trades in its local currency. The combined equity is converted to AUD, including the FX move, only at the portfolio/reporting layer.

      ⚠ Paper trading on real prices — places no real orders. A backtest is a hypothesis, not a promise.
